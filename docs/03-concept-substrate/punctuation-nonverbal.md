# Punctuation: the Non-Verbal Channel NSM Omits

A distinct, load-bearing part of the current primitive-functions work: the communication channel
that has **no direct verbal (word/prime) expression**, which punctuation explicates in writing.

Source: claim 260 (Patrick-direct).

---

## What NSM leaves out

> *NSM assumes / subsumes the elements of communication that have **no** direct verbal (word/prime)
> expression, and **punctuation** is what explicates them in writing.* — claim 260

NSM models the verbal/word channel. But a great deal of communication is carried by a separate
**paralinguistic / non-verbal channel** — pause, intonation, emphasis, prosody, mood markers,
body language. In writing, that channel surfaces as **punctuation:**

| Glyph | Non-verbal function it transcribes |
|-------|------------------------------------|
| comma `,` | pause |
| period `.` | full stop / boundary |
| question mark `?` | interrogative intonation / body-language marker |
| exclamation `!` | emphasis / intensity |

These are written **symbols for pause and body-language-based delimiters.** They are a real
communication channel and they carry Theory of Mind (claim 200, all-expression-conveys-ToM) — but
NSM omits them because it models only the verbal channel. Without them, the
[explication-as-db-instructions](explication.md) picture is **missing the non-verbal channel
entirely.** They must be specified to sit **beside** the prime/content ops.

---

## They are function-universal across modes — the multi-modal key

The important architectural point: these elements are **function-universal across expression
modes.** The same non-verbal delimiter surfaces as:

- **punctuation** in text,
- **prosody / intonation** in speech,
- **gesture / body language** in person.

Extracting the *function* from under the punctuation glyph (the same English-mining extraction as
claim 257) yields a **mode-spanning communication element** that the
[conceptual hub](../02-architecture/linguistic-vs-conceptual.md) shares across text / speech /
embodied expression. English punctuation is simply the text-surface where this non-verbal channel
is *already explicitly written down* — which is exactly why it is the place to mine it.

---

## Not "syntax glue" — the embodied channel

> This claim explicitly **supersedes** an earlier over-formalized framing that called these
> "structural operators" / combinators. Punctuation *is* structural, but its **nature** is the
> transcribed non-verbal channel, not CS-style syntax glue (claim 260).

The right cut is the **embodied / non-verbal** channel, not computer-science syntax. Comma is a
pause, not a separator token; the question mark is interrogative *intonation*, not a query
operator. Keeping this distinction straight is what lets the function generalize cleanly to speech
prosody and physical gesture in later modalities (Phase 4, see [../../ROADMAP.md](../../ROADMAP.md)).
